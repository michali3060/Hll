How to play

New player mistakes

  • Not listening to officers and the commander - whenever they tell you to defend you should listen to them. Golden rule of Hell Let Loose is Defence > offense. It's better to stay in the same place and defend than lose your point while you're far out attacking.

  • Being far out in the enemy field - there's no need to go that far if you're not recon. Wandering around for no reason is not the best tactic.

  • Running across open fields - Don't worry you're not gonna be late. Going around wouldn't take much more time but it can save your life.

  • Going solo in a tank - There's plenty of space in there for a guy or two. Would be faster if you didn't have to switch seats and restart your engine over and over again when you want to move the vehicle.

  • Choosing officer as your first role - one step at a time. Don't overestimate yourself, choose rifleman first. The greatest officers started as privates too.

  • Moving too much - sometimes it's better to stay in one place and wait for the enemy.

  • Shooting fast without aiming - one good shot is better than many bad ones.

  • Killing the people in blue - sometimes they may act like enemy but it's not always the case.

  • Drunk driving - never do it, it's dangerous and illegal.

Good player habits

  • Checking map often - it's important to know what's happening on the battlefield. Better to know if your teammates don't need your help.

  • Checking the surroundings before moving - you should always feel like somebody's watching you. Death comes at moments when you least expect it.

  • Using a microphone - It's faster than writing. Every second costs.

  • Building a lot of garrisons - isn't it great when you can quickly spawn in every place on the map? Just keep it in the limit of eight.

  • Building nodes as soon as possible and surrounding them with barbed wire - nodes give resources every minute. Surrounding them with barbed wire prevents the enemy from destroying them quickly.

  • Using your ears - you really thought you don't have to? Blasting music won't help you hear the enemy coming.
